⏱️ The First 3 Seconds: Make or Break

80% of viewers decide in the first 3 seconds whether to keep watching.

Your hook must do ONE thing: pattern interrupt. Break their scroll.

6 Hook Patterns That Work

1. Question Hook
"Are you still wasting money on Facebook ads?"
Why it works: Brain automatically tries to answer. Creates engagement.
2. Shock/Contradiction Hook
"I spent $50,000 on ads and made ZERO profit. Here's what I learned."
Why it works: Unexpected. Creates curiosity about the mistake.
3. Result Hook (Numbers)
"We went from 2x to 8x ROAS in 30 days. Here's how."
Why it works: Specific numbers = believability. Promises answer.
4. Direct Address Hook
"If you're running Facebook ads and your ROAS is below 3x, watch this."
Why it works: Speaks directly to target audience. Creates relevance.
5. POV/Story Hook
"POV: You just lost $1000 on a Facebook ad that didn't convert."
Why it works: Emotional connection. Viewer sees themselves.
6. Visual Pattern Interrupt
[Fast cuts, zoom in, sudden movement] + "Stop scrolling."
Why it works: Movement breaks the scroll. Demands attention.

📝 The 15-Second Script Formula

This is the structure of every high-converting video ad. Copy it exactly.

0:00-0:03 | HOOK
Pattern interrupt. Get attention.
"Are you wasting money on Facebook ads?"
0:03-0:08 | PROBLEM
State the pain. Agitate it.
"Most people get 2x ROAS and think it's good. But you're actually losing money after product costs and fees."
0:08-0:12 | SOLUTION
Present your product/service as the fix.
"Our tool finds winning hooks in 24 hours. Guaranteed 4x ROAS or money back."
0:12-0:15 | CTA
Tell them exactly what to do next.
"Click below. Try it free for 14 days."

🎬 Editing Rules (The 5 Non-Negotiables)

Rule #1: Cut Every 2-3 Seconds

Attention span is 2 seconds on mobile. New angle/cut every 2-3 seconds keeps attention.

Rule #2: Text Overlays (Always)

85% watch without sound. Key points must be on-screen as text.

Rule #3: No Intro (Start Mid-Sentence)

No "Hey guys, today I'm gonna..." Start with the hook immediately.

Rule #4: Music = 30% Volume

Background music yes. But voice must be 70% louder. Music should be subtle.

Rule #5: 9:16 Vertical (Mobile First)

Square (1:1) is dead. Vertical takes up full screen on mobile = better performance.

Quick Editing Checklist

  • ✅ Hook in first 3 seconds
  • ✅ Cut every 2-3 seconds
  • ✅ Text overlays on key points
  • ✅ No intro/outro
  • ✅ CTA in last 3 seconds
  • ✅ 9:16 vertical format
  • ✅ Music at 30% volume
  • ✅ Total length: 10-20 seconds

Format Decision Tree

If you sell physical products: → UGC
If you sell services/coaching: → Talking Head
If you sell software/app: → Screen Recording + Voiceover
If you need trust: → Customer Testimonial
If product is complex: → Animated Explainer

🎯 CTA Techniques That Force Clicks

Your video is perfect. But if the CTA is weak, no one clicks.

Technique #1: Point to the Button

Literally point at the "Learn More" button. Visual cue = higher CTR.

"Click the button below. Try it free."

Technique #2: Create Urgency

Time-limited or scarcity-based CTA.

"Only 10 spots left this month. Click now."

Technique #3: Risk Reversal

Remove all risk. Make it a no-brainer.

"Try it free for 14 days. Cancel anytime. No credit card."

Technique #4: Social Proof CTA

Show that others already did it.

"Join 5,000+ marketers. Click below."

Technique #5: Curiosity CTA

Promise the answer if they click.

"Click to see the full case study."
